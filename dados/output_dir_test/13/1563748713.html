9 new COVID-19 cases reported in Schuylkill County

  • Staff report
  • Mar 30, 2020
  • Mar 30, 2020 Updated Jul 14, 2020

Schuylkill County now has 30 COVID-19 cases, with nine new cases reported by the state Department of Health on Monday.

The jump from 21 was the largest single day increase since the first case was reported in the county on March 22.

Luzerne County, where a stay at home order was issued Friday, has jumped to 150 cases, with 56 added to its total today. Luzerne County posted its third death from the coronavirus. It had two deaths last week.

Statewide there are 693 new cases, a one-day high, bringing the total to 4,087 positive cases. Officially, there have been 49 deaths as a result of the virus.

Philadelphia (1,007) and Montgomery (540) lead the state in countywide cases. The five-county region near Philadelphia has accounted for 55% of the COVID-19 cases in the state and 20 deaths.

Most cases are seen in the 25-49 age group (41%). Next is ages 50-64 (28%), followed by ages 65-plus (19%) and ages 19-24 (10%). Children 18 and under make up about 1% of cases. The state has had 386 hospitalizations since March 6.

For most people, the virus causes only mild or moderate symptoms, such as fever and cough. For some, especially older adults and people with existing health problems, it can cause more severe illness, including pneumonia. The vast majority of people recover.
