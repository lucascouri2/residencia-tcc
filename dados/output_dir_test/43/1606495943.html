You can donate all those empties to a good cause

With many stores not accepting them due to pandemic restrictions, empty returnable cans and beer bottles are piling up in Quebecers garages.

On the next three Saturdays, some Montreal-area IGA, Metro and Super C grocery stores will set up drop-off points for the returnables in their parking lots, with money raised going to charity.

The first donation day Saturday, May 16 will take place in seven boroughs: Ahuntsic-Cartierville, Lachine, LaSalle, Rivire-des-PrairiesPointe-aux-Trembles, St-Laurent, Ville-Marie and VilleraySaint-MichelParc-Extension.

They will be open from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.

The collections are a joint effort by the grocery store chains and Jour de la Terre, organizer of Quebec Earth Day events.

Quebec delays reopening Montreal schools, records 131 new deaths

Schools in Greater Montreal will not reopen until late August/early September, Premier Franois Legault announced this afternoon, describing the situation in the region as fragile.

Children had been scheduled to return to class on May 25.

He made the announcement at a joint press conference with Mayor Valrie Plante.

I think it was the right thing to do, Plante said of the delay.

Quebec high schools are currently holding classes online, as are CEGEPs and universities. CEGEPs and universities have said most classes will be held online in the fall.

Legault said he hopes high schools, CEGEPs and universities will hold at least some classes in person in the new school year.

The reopening of daycares, also slated for May 25, is being delayed until at least June 1, the premier announced.

Legault said the provincial government has not yet decided whether non-essential businesses in Greater Montreal will be allowed to reopen as scheduled on May 25.

It will take a few days to decide whether businesses can reopen, he said.

I am convinced that we can regain control of the situation in Montreal.

Legault added that its important that Montrealers wear masks (when leaving home), especially if we want to open businesses on May 25.

He said Quebec may make facial coverings obligatory on public transit.

  • Quebec has 40,724 cases of COVID-19, an increase of 793 from yesterday.
  • The provincial death toll is now 3,351, an increase of 131.
  • Montreal Island alone has 20,633 cases and 2,154 deaths.

Anxiety higher among Quebec immigrants, visible minorities

On Wednesday, results of an Association for Canadian Studies poll indicated that English-speaking Quebecers are more likely than francophones to be afraid of contracting COVID-19 and more likely to know an infected person.

When Premier Franois Legault was asked about the findings, he blamed the anglophone media, singling out the Montreal Gazette.

Advertisement 13

Story continues below
This advertisement has not loaded yet, but your article continues below.

Article content

Today, ACS president Jack Jedwab has published more details, this time focusing on Quebec immigrants and visible minorities.

He found that they are also more afraid of the disease than the provincial average.

Jedwab said immigrants are:

  • More anxious than non-immigrants, especially immigrants who have been in Canada for less than 10 years.
  • Far more likely to feel the economic impact of the virus andto want to slow the pace of de-confinement.
  • More likely than non-immigrants to be worried about a family member getting the virus.
  • More likely to know someone diagnosed with the virus.

The findings also show that:

  • Visible minorities are far more likely than others to fear getting the virus.
  • Quebec Jews are far more likely to be afraid of getting the virus than Christians and atheists.
  • Quebecers who are most anxious about the novel coronavirus have adhered most closely to the governments advice on preventive measures.

11:30 a.m.

Canada partially reopening national parks in June, Trudeau says

Prime Minister Justin Trudeau this morning announced $470 million in emergency financial aid for fish harvesters.

Those who expect a 25-per-cent drop in income this season will get up to $10,000 to cover losses, he said. Ottawa will also provide non-repayable grants of up to $10,000 for fish harvesters who own a business.

And employment insurance benefits will be beefed up so harvesters can apply based on last years revenues, Trudeau said.

He also announced some good news for the weeks ahead on national parks.

As of the beginning of June, some national parks will be partially reopening, allowing Canadians to use some trails and green spaces where physical distancing is possible.

TORONTO Ontario is reporting 258 new cases of COVID-19 today and 33 more deaths.

That brings Ontario to a total of 21,494 cases, a 1.2-per-cent increase over the previous day the lowest growth rate since early March.

Bank of Canada warns of excess debt from COVID-19

From The Canadian Press:

OTTAWA The Bank of Canada says there are signs in the countrys financial markets that suggest concern about the ability of companies to weather the COVID-19 economic crisis.

The central bank has spent the last two months making a flurry of policy decisions that has seen it slash its target interest rate and embark on an unprecedented bond-buying program to ease the flow of credit.

The report suggests these measures have helped ease liquidity strains and provide easy access to short-term credit for companies and households.

Six out of 10 Canadian COVID-19 deaths were in Quebec
